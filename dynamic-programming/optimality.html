Optimality

Value functions define a partial ordering over policies.

Definition

A policy π\piπ is defined to be better than or equal to a policy π′\pi'π′ if its expected return is greater than or equal to that of π′\pi'π′ over all states.

In other words,

π≥π′  ⟺  vπ(s)≥vπ′(s) ∀s\Large \pi \geq \pi' \iff v_{\pi}(s) \geq v_{\pi'}(s)\ \forall sπ≥π′⟺vπ​(s)≥vπ′​(s) ∀s

The optimal policy is the one that is better than or equal to all the other policies. There may be multiple optimal policies. We denote it by v∗v_{*}v∗​.

Optimal policies also share the same optimal action-value function, denoted by q∗q_{*}q∗​.

For the state-action pair (s,a)(s, a)(s,a), this gives the expected return for taking the action aaa in state sss and thereafter following an optimal policy.

v∗(s)=max⁡πvπ(s) q∗(s,a)=max⁡πqπ(s,a)\large v_{*}(s) = \max_{\pi} v_{\pi}(s) \\ \ \\ q_{*}(s, a) = \max_{\pi} q_{\pi}(s, a)v∗​(s)=πmax​vπ​(s) q∗​(s,a)=πmax​qπ​(s,a)

In any finite MDP, there is always at least one deterministic optimal policy.
